Qui Sui

Ambiguous.

That's what a beginning is to me.

How and why does one start writing in a journal such as this? What compels someone to begin a thing so unremarkably ambitious? Why fix the natural mistakes and uncertainties that come from being human if there is no audience to witness them, criticize them? What purpose does this serve? I don't intend to answer more questions than I can pose, but I wish they could be as simple as I need them to be. Instead they, both question and answer, live in the same world as beginnings, and ends for that matter.

Stark profundity evades my weak person, which I truly am. I fear the ramblings of a child and that of a mad_man differ little from each other, and almost as little as they differ from a stream like this. But is childish insanity a symptom of immaturity, or just the nature of thought? Again, if I could answer then I would be Him. The only distinctly unambiguous things I feel are the connections that overwhelm my mind. Coherence is sacrificed to capture a more honest picture of the self, which requires abandoning these connections and letting the mad-house-bound mind explore the space it never feels it may. I suppose that is the cure for insanity - freedom to disconnect.

If only more things could be de-ambiguated by the mind alone, but alas.

The honesty needed to be free for that can't be learned, nor can anything that's poised to be as satisfying to the self. Instead it's already in the self, just lying dormant waiting for the chance to breathe unimpeded, unmolested, and, most important, un-unambiguously. That's why there can't be a satisfyin conclusion, just as there can be no satisfying end. The desperate search for it falls victim to the winding paths that endowed it with awareness and feeling to begin with. And the search, just like the questions, can't rest here pristined,
